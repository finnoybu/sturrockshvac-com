## Priority 1 — Google Reviews

Competitors in the map pack have 250+ Google reviews. Sturrock's currently has
zero on Google. This is the fastest-moving SEO signal, and once the GBP is
verified (~5–7 days after you submit the claim), you can start immediately.

### What to do

1. Google gives verified profiles a short review link (`g.page/r/...`).
   Save that link — it's what you send customers.

2. **Text every past happy customer** with the link. Suggested message:

   > Hi [Name], it's Andy at Sturrock's. Would you be willing to leave a
   > quick Google review about your [service]? It really helps small
   > businesses like ours. Here's the link: [your review link]. Thanks!

3. **Systematize for future jobs**: text the link within 24 hours of every
   completed service call. This should become as automatic as emailing an
   invoice.

4. **Respond to every review** — good and bad. Google rewards engagement.

**Target**: 20 reviews in 90 days is a realistic first milestone.

## Priority 4 — Local link building (slower, compounds)

Harder but valuable for long-term ranking:

- Sponsor a local little league team or community event — most sponsorships
  include a website link in a sponsors page.
- Write a short guest post for a Loudoun/Hillsboro community blog about
  something HVAC-related ("why you should schedule fall tune-ups," etc.).
- Check supplier websites (Carrier, Trane, Lennox, etc.) for contractor-finder
  pages where you can list Sturrock's.
- Ask long-standing business partners if they'll link to Sturrock's from their
  sites.

---

## Priority 5 — Measurement

Once the GBP is verified and we have some traffic data flowing:

1. **Cloudflare Web Analytics**: Free, privacy-first (no cookies, no
   banner), enabled in the Cloudflare dashboard. Gives pageviews,
   referrers, top pages, and rough geographic breakdowns.
2. **Google Search Console**: Free, from Google. Shows which search queries
   the site appears for and where we rank. Sitemap is already submitted at
   <https://sturrockshvac.com/sitemap.xml>.
3. **Conversion events** (phone clicks, form submissions) are not currently
   tracked. If we later want that data, we'd add a paid tool like Plausible
   or re-introduce GA4.
